ESSAY - draft 01.

questions //for now//: who is dreaming of whom? are images producing stories or are we producing stories onto images? - how does lack of visual/known information influence narrative and story telling in image making? Optic - Haptic, linguistic - atmospheric? BOLD ITALIC - super stupid sounding phrasing i'd like to still change. It must have been months, of constant work with piles and piles of imagery, collected over a stretch of time, way too long for my liking. Surely, it started nicely organised and collected in a pocket notebook, with print outs, cut outs and simply acquired scraps, but so quickly got out of hand, now spilling itself out "new folder" numbered 21, 22, 23 and so on. Some titled simply "screenshot" or "image 1", after going on one of those mindless scavengings with three buttons as a haunting tool and four windows of open internet archive tabs. And so they would grow and deform and just when I thought I was done, some more would follow. Five months, counting from the last days of August until sometime, beginning of January, where it seemed appropriate to look, with a bit too much complacency, at the consequences of these rummagings. the problem wasn't the overwhelming amounts, or the unknown origins, it was rather the rough, eye-catching edges, and awful reds and greens fighting with each other in a way they only could, still trapped on my computer screen, in it's full bright an glory. still too familiar, as if proud of where they come from. leaving an unsatisfactory ring to it's keeper ears. they were now no longer permitted (authorised) to such obscenities and it was my role to strip them out of it. put them in rows of three, sometimes four, fitted perfectly within borders of a single spread sheet. sent through multiple check-points, first removing their name and later on, colour. they started spitting out one by one. followed by lining them up once more, this time to scan through, revealing the dotted pattern forming instead of greys and blacks, followed by grains of paper hidden behind thick layers of ink. what i was left with now, was a complete. a whole, built carefully from lack to a meaning, a reflection and a myth. it was only a matter of order, that would make them recite their stories back at you in a newly found eagerness. what first began as a series of processes, was now a way of dealing with visual material as close to one of those tricks where you pull endless fabric out of your left sleeve, or pull cards out of a companion's ear. maybe it came from taking too much inspiration from one of my preferred reads - those of Elena and Mikhail Bulgakov or John Fowles, and this desire of creating myths around work with simple schemes that, once uncovered seemed almost amusing. i like to think there is a place for humour and irony, under those grey collages and pages of texts i spat out over the last years. what if i were to say, the carefully edited crops of images only make sense, because you want there to be a sense to make? Removing layers of narrative, paradoxically mirrors your desire of there being one, so it gets spilled out, filling the holes that are leftover. Almost shamefully, there i was circling back to blurred out images of light strobes, levitating tables, gauzes spilling out of opened mouths and candles lighting up the rooms. the more shaken, uncertain, they were, the more i kept staring at them. dream like, they told me their stories and my future - unintentional ritual. as curious as I ought to have been, no questions where asked out loud, subconsciously deemed as unnecessary. i was not there to undermine it's methods, trace back their origins. same as you would be shamed for catching a magician off-stage, to pull on his sleeve in search of the end of the never-ending fabric he pulled in the spot-lights a second ago. what matters to you now is what you have witnessed with your own two eyes, the goosebumps that ran down your spine making your hair stand up in a peculiar mix of fear and excitement. maybe your subconscious has exceeded your eyes, whispering down your ear the logistics of that ghostly figure hidden in the left corner of the photograph, but the point is that it does not matter anymore. The mysticism finally surpasses reason in a dream like ritual of viewing and being viewed. "Phantasmagoria" rented on Internet Archive, on page 309, you would find the twenty fourth chapter titled: "The Rorschach Test; or Dirty Pictures", which word by word, walks you through the reflective quality of ambiguous imagery. ink splatters mirrored, by the folding of a paper sheet form a mirror of a human desire to see meaning where there is none - pareidolia. Alongside the image. A haunting mimetic ghost of both yourself and the image in front. sometimes leaning over the line of insanity, if taken as such, often in the case of the Rorschach test itself, to determine layers of obscenity displayed by patients treated in psychological facilities. toning it down though, gave us an ordinary cognitive process to allow digestions of art and sound in relation to oneself. comparable to those acts of palm-readings and fortune tellings, where in a subliminal space, perception is being questioned and re-introduced by simple tactics and tricks. i think back to my rows of collected grey-scale prints, obviously treated. is the method, obvious to a bare eye, convincing enough to transform clear data and language of it's original form, into something close to fantasia, dream state, spectacle? the more deformation is added to what was primarily a perfectly clear stream of informations and signs, the more it demands from the one submitted to it's perception. Doing a reverse procedure to that of a poet Justinus Koerner, who's taken accidental marks left on margins of letter paragraphs and painted on more meaningful lines, equipping them with the ability of telling his particular story and narrative, with no room for further interpretation. he gave those splotters a more literary sense, by adding on information, whereas arguably in the process of degradation, i aim to add a subliminal layer that is utterly dependent on it's perceiver. The image is no longer a linguistic document, but an element of fantasia - an atmospheric perception, that of lack from optical information. in short, we come to the point of the conclusion that has to be made, by me and by the reader, to achieve meaning out of a cluster, a collection of spilled out thoughts, dreams, notes and images. i started with the process of creating, so to finish i'll come back to it. this text has been written in order to understand the mechanism of a not-so-conscious scheme used by the writer (me) to practice story telling through image making. i can control the preparatory steps (surely that gives me some kind of authorship), but that is until they paradoxically gain autonomy through my imprisonment. lesson learned quickly, as through production of a visual research document - i was led on by this foolish cycle, waiting for it to resolve my confusion and getting nothing less then an amplification of it. rows of seemingly identical prints, upon further investigation couldn't have been any more different from each other now. it goes as stated before: scavenging, imprisoning, stripping, unifying, re-arranging.

*dreams

"I called them two, three and seven, which used to be eight, now eleven, twelve and finally thirteen"

a recurring dream, The Hague, August 2025.

I didn't remember the specifics of my First Arrival. No opening of doors, or crawling through any gates or curtains—no long corridors prior to entering the Living Room. Sheepishly lit by what felt like rows of partialy mlted candles, it revealed walls of crooked wooden planks. The longer I stared at them, the longer and further away they seemed to stand. I quickly averted my eyes, scared that if I lingered too long, I'd welcome in the blanks of the lines running up the planks, and I didn't feel like leaving the Living Room yet. One window. One chair. Both placed in the middle—carefully, with signs of continuous use marking up the velvet fabric. Sunken sides on each armrest and a greyed-out spot where your spine would lie down seeking rest. An urge rose up: one, two steps and I'll be molding my tired, weighed-down body perfectly onto the seat in front of me. I hesitated instead, and what felt like a need was now gone. I could move freely again. Thirteen picture frames, hung on a single nail and a piece of hemp twine. Some of them hanging lower than others, tilted to one side or another. It was hard to make out anything from the center of the Living Room, and right there, even before my hip could tense up to make a move, I realized—a revelation, a distant memory, which was what I have always known. Of course, I thought. Obviously I haven't said it, as I'm not yet able to make out letters or words. My dear, all thirteen of them. One, two, and three, and then what four was five, eleven, twelve that was nine. Today those were their names, and would be their names until tomorrow's countdown comes again. For a second, when it still feels so unfamiliar, I try to bring back the real ones. Green fields and blue skies. How silly of me? I don't bother to give it a second longer, though. It doesn't make them any less, and it doesn't make me any less, either. There is no representation and her origins, just because I haven't learned these terms yet. All I know is right there, in front of me, framed and hung on the walls. I'm met again with thirteen. Grey and white and black. Sometimes, when I've been sitting in the chair for too long, facing away, I catch them looking similar or even the same, but then I remember that they are mine and I know better. It all lasts no longer than half a blink of an eye, or what I'm assuming half a blink of an eye would last. Sometimes I leave the living room, and when I'm back things are different— and they only become the same after I've entered it again. The Third Time I stepped in, something else became apparent. There it was, a presence I had carefully omitted the Times before. I have known my thirteen, but I forgot about what succeeded them. I came closer to today's eight. The dark frame around was, as it has always been, but there was something that wasn't there before. Glass spilled over the image behind it, reflecting the flickers of fiery candle knots, but not only. There stood a reflection of what could only be the one of myself. I knew I felt, experienced, saw, and walked before—that was sure. However, what makes the rules of the Living Room is the absence of conclusions formed at times. Naturally, with the time spent inside, you might start to connect and conclude, and it will eventually fade into embedded knowledge, so you will forget it again. Here, only intuition echoes, and the former things, if there are any, exist only upon reflection possible outside. Outside, where the green grass and blue skies lie, with terms and books full of words and descriptions. Obviously, there was no physical shape or form to seek out. And since the only thing I knew to be mine was what I put up on walls and counted and cared for, it must have been what I were to become. So there I was again, now the same as ever, but it was the Living Room that felt unfamiliar. Frames higher than before, copper badges below each. The armchair, almost repelling to look at, unfamiliar. The Fourth Time: hollow thuds of my legs entering filled the space, causing the frames to quiver slightly. My neck still, taking in all four walls simultaneously now. I could feel a short absence—that of the pristine chair—before it became forgotten again. It was when what would only be a cluster of sounds rolled off my tongue in an attempt to say my own name. I recognized it from the copper badges, from the Times before, as I didn't possess the ability to read anymore. In short anticipation I twitch my ears and wait for them to echo back to me, but feel no disappointment when they never do. My front knees bend in half, followed by the pair behind, and I fall to the floor. There, in the corner of my left eye, I feel the wailing call of the armchair again, so I push my hands off the floor and walk towards it. There I was, the Fourth Time as always, sat in the middle, resting back my sore spine, with thumbs grazing on each side of the chair, legs crossed. It was almost time to give them today's names, I had to remind myself.
